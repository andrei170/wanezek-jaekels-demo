Heritage

Why 1908 matters in Green Bay law

Heritage is the one credential that cannot be acquired, manufactured, or duplicated. In a market where every firm claims to be "trusted" or "experienced," 118 unbroken years is the only number that ends the comparison.

M.E. Davis opened the doors of this firm in 1908, four years after graduating from Marquette University Law School. He could not have known that the practice would still be running on Adams Street in 2026, but that is the fact on the table. The firm has been continuously operated under one professional roof for 118 years.

The Green Bay legal market has dozens of capable firms. A few are decades old. A small handful trace founders back into the mid-20th century. None of them have an unbroken century-plus operating record in this city. Per the firm's own About page, Wanezek, Jaekels, Daul & Babcock is the longest continuously operated law firm in Green Bay. That sentence is a verifiable competitive moat.

What 118 years buys, in practical terms: institutional memory of how Brown County courts move, partner relationships with Wisconsin trial judges who started their careers facing this firm, and a paper trail of real estate, business, and probate matters that traces multiple generations of local families. New firms do not have that. Out-of-town firms cannot rent it.

The problem, on the public web, is that none of it shows up. Search "oldest law firm Green Bay" or "law firm Green Bay 1908" and the answer engines hand back generic directory listings. The 118-year story exists, but no one has built the infrastructure to make it citable.

What we would build

Four moves, in order. No theory. Just the build.

The work is direct. Translate the 118-year story into structured data that answer engines actually read, then build per-attorney and per-practice pages that compound on that authority. None of it requires the firm to change how it practices law.

Step 01

Founder & firm-history page

A dedicated page covering M.E. Davis, the 1904 Marquette Law credential, the founding in 1908, and the unbroken chain of stewardship across 118 years. Cited dates, named partners, and verifiable claims.

Step 02

Per-attorney schema

Structured Attorney/Person JSON-LD entities for David Daul, Greg Babcock, Jeffrey Jaekels, Nathan Mueller, and Warren Wanezek. Each attorney becomes individually citable in AI search and Google's knowledge layer.

Step 03

Practice-area funnels

Seven dedicated practice pages (family, estate, probate, business, litigation, real estate, PI). Each one a true landing page with intent-matched copy and an intake path, not a thin directory entry.

Step 04

SCR-compliant disclaimers

Every page, every claim, and every per-attorney biography reviewed against Wisconsin Supreme Court Rules 20:7.1 and 20:7.4 governing attorney advertising. Heritage claims sourced; specialization language calibrated.

Why now

The 118-year window is not infinite

Heritage is the one asset that grows automatically with time, and decays only when newer firms get better at the search surface than you do. Right now, that decay is happening on every "best law firm" query in Brown County. Younger firms with smaller war chests but better technical infrastructure are eating citation share that, on the facts, belongs to a 1908 founding.

The fix is not bigger budgets or more advertising. The fix is making the verifiable facts of this firm machine-readable. A 1908 founding date, a Marquette 1904 credential, five named partners with defined practice mixes, and an Adams Street office that has held that address for the life of the firm. None of those facts need to be invented. They need to be encoded.

Every quarter the answer surface gets harder to enter. Models lock in the firms that arrived early. The firms that show up first in AI overviews in 2027 will be the firms that built proper schema and citable history pages in 2026.

FAQ

Direct answers to the questions a prospective client actually asks

How long has Wanezek, Jaekels, Daul & Babcock been operating?
The firm was founded in 1908 by M.E. Davis on Adams Street in Green Bay, and has operated continuously at that address ever since. As of 2026 that is 118 unbroken years, which makes it, per the firm's own About page, the longest continuously operated law firm in Green Bay.
Who is M.E. Davis?
M.E. Davis was the founding attorney of the firm. He graduated from Marquette University Law School in 1904 and opened the Green Bay practice in 1908. The firm has been operated by successive Wisconsin attorneys ever since, with the current name reflecting Warren Wanezek, Jeffrey Jaekels, David Daul, and Greg Babcock as the firm's named partners.
What practice areas does the firm cover?
Seven core practice areas: family law, estate planning, probate, business law, civil and commercial litigation, real estate, and personal injury. Each partner anchors a defined mix, with Greg Babcock leading family law and bankruptcy, David Daul leading personal injury and employment matters, Jeffrey Jaekels covering commercial litigation and construction, and Nathan Mueller handling elder law, estate, and real estate matters.
